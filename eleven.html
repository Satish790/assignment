Text Formating

HTML Text Formating.

    1.bold (b)
  1. Use to make text stand out or emphasize a point
  2. Do not use for entire paragraphs or long blocks of text
  3. Avoid overusing, as it can make text difficult to read
    2.italic (i)
  1. Use to indicate emphasis or to differentiate between words or phrases
  2. Can be used for book or movie titles, foreign words or phrases, or to denote thoughts or quotes within a sentence
  3. Avoid overusing, as it can become distracting or lose its impact
    3.strong
  1. Use to indicate importance or significance of text
  2. Can be used to highlight key points, warnings, or notices
  3. Can be combined with other formatting tags, such as bold or italic, to create emphasis
    4.emphasis (em)
  1. Similar to italic, use to indicate emphasis or to differentiate between words or phrases
  2. Can be used for book or movie titles, foreign words or phrases, or to denote thoughts or quotes within a sentence
  3. Can also be used to indicate a change in tone or mood
    5.Underline
  1. Use to emphasize a single word or phrase
  2. Avoid using for entire blocks of text, as it can make it difficult to read
  3. Consider using bold or italic instead for emphasis
